Impact of Iron Deficiency Anemia on Inpatient Outcomes in Inflammatory Bowel Disease

Analysis of NIS 2017-2022 Data

Author
Affiliation

Muhammad Saad

Dow University of Health Sciences

Preamble:

  • Reference Studies:

    • Saad et al., 2026
    • Alharbi et al., 2024

  • Study Objective:

    To investigate the clinical and economic impact of Iron Deficiency Anemia (IDA) in patients hospitalized with Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD). Specifically, this study evaluates whether comorbid IDA is independently associated with higher in-hospital mortality, increased healthcare utilization (length of stay, inflation-adjusted costs), need for inpatient blood transfusions, and adverse discharge disposition.

  • Data Source:

    A retrospective cohort study of the 2017–2022 Nationwide Inpatient Sample (NIS), developed by the Healthcare Cost and Utilization Project (HCUP). The NIS is the largest publicly available all-payer inpatient database in the United States, representing a 20% stratified sample of discharges from U.S. community hospitals and enabling generation of national estimates through survey-weighted analyses.

  • Cohort Definition:

    Index hospitalizations were included if they met all of the following criteria:

    • Adults aged ≥18 years
    • Diagnosis of Inflammatory Bowel Disease (Crohn’s Disease or Ulcerative Colitis), identified using ICD-10-CM codes K50.x and K51.x

  • Exposure Categories:

    Iron Deficiency Anemia (IDA) status, identified using ICD-10-CM codes D50.x:

    • IBD without IDA: Absence of IDA diagnosis codes (reference group)
    • IBD with IDA: Presence of IDA (D50.x)

  • Outcomes of Interest:

    • Primary Outcomes:

      • In-hospital mortality
      • Inpatient blood transfusion

    • Secondary Outcomes:

      • Length of stay (LOS, days)
      • Inflation-adjusted total hospital costs (U.S. dollars, adjusted to 2022 using CPI)
      • Non-home discharge (transfer to skilled nursing facility, long-term care, hospice, or in-hospital death)

  • Covariates and Variable Construction:

    • Demographic & Socioeconomic Factors:

      • Age (continuous, years)
      • Sex (Male/Female)
      • Race/ethnicity (White, Black, Hispanic, Asian or Pacific Islander, Other)
      • Primary expected payer (Medicare, Medicaid, Private, Other)
      • ZIP-based median household income quartile (Q1–Q4)

    • Hospital Characteristics:

      • Hospital region (Northeast, Midwest, South, West)
      • Bed size (Small, Medium, Large)
      • Location and teaching status (Rural, Urban non-teaching, Urban teaching)

    • Clinical Characteristics:

      • Admission type (Elective vs. Non-elective)
      • IBD Phenotype (Crohn’s Disease vs. Ulcerative Colitis)
      • Elixhauser Comorbidity Index (continuous sum score)
      • Selected baseline confounders: Clostridioides difficile infection, liver disease, renal failure, diabetes mellitus, hypertension, obesity

  • Statistical Methods:

    • Survey Design and Weighting:

      • Analyses incorporated NIS’s complex sampling design using discharge weights (DISCWT), strata (NIS_STRATUM), and clustering (HOSP_NIS). The survey package in R was used to generate nationally representative estimates.

    • Descriptive Statistics:

      • Baseline characteristics and outcomes were summarized by IDA status (IBD without IDA vs. IBD with IDA).

      • Design-based statistical tests: Rao–Scott adjusted chi-square test for categorical variables; design-based t-test/Wilcoxon for continuous variables.

    • Multivariable Regression:

      • Binary outcomes:

        • Survey-weighted logistic regression with quasibinomial family
        • Adjusted odds ratios (aORs) with 95% confidence intervals

      • Continuous outcomes:

        • Survey-weighted linear regression with gaussian family
        • Adjusted beta coefficients (β) with 95% confidence intervals

      • Adjustment variables: Age, sex, race/ethnicity, primary payer, income quartile, hospital region, bed size, teaching status, admission type (elective vs. non-elective), IBD phenotype (Crohn’s Disease vs. Ulcerative Colitis), and the Elixhauser Comorbidity Index sum score.

      • Reference group: IBD without IDA

  • Software:

    All analyses were conducted in R Statistical Language (Version 4.5.1; R Foundation for Statistical Computing, Vienna, Austria).
